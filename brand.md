# WonderLoom brand system

WonderLoom is a calm, child-led creative studio where a child can speak an idea, see it become tangible, revise it, and finish a small storybook that still feels unmistakably theirs.

## Promise

The child is the author. WonderLoom makes their thinking visible and helps it travel farther.

## Voice

- Warm, specific, and economical.
- Curious without interrogating.
- Invitations, never commands: “What should happen next?” and “You can choose one, or make your own.”
- The AI is “your Creative Guide,” never a friend, companion, therapist, or authority.
- Revision is celebrated as normal creative work.
- Silence, skipping, rejecting, and undoing are always valid.

## Visual language

- Warm paper rather than white software chrome.
- Deep aubergine ink as the primary accent.
- Coral, teal, saffron, and lilac appear only as idea categories and authorship signals.
- Cut-paper shapes, soft fiber, uneven edges, and restrained depth make the world feel hand-made.
- Editorial asymmetry gives each page a composed, storybook quality.
- Generated scene art contains no typography; interface labels remain crisp and accessible around it.

## Brand mark

- The primary mark is the tactile paper fox at `public/images/brand/wonderloom-fox-mark-v1.png`.
- Use it as a transparent PNG on warm paper surfaces; preserve its aspect ratio and clear space.
- The fox is a workshop guidepost, not an autonomous character speaking for the child.
- Do not place the mark inside a generic gradient badge or add text inside the image.

## Typography

- Fraunces: expressive display and storybook titles.
- Nunito Sans: readable interface, prompts, controls, and book copy.
- Large type is reserved for meaning, not decoration.

## Interaction principles

1. Direct manipulation: a contribution becomes a visible card or change immediately.
2. Causal motion: movement explains where an idea came from and where it went.
3. Interruptibility: audio can be stopped, edits can be undone, suggestions can be ignored.
4. One question at a time: the canvas is the primary object; the guide never floods it with chat.
5. Honest states: listening, thinking, painting, ready, and blocked are explicit.
6. Accessible calm: no streaks, timers, confetti, infinite feeds, urgency, or engagement traps.

## Authorship language

- Child contribution: coral thread and “Your idea.”
- Creative Guide suggestion: lilac thread and “Guide suggestion.”
- Adult-supported input: teal thread and “Grown-up helped.”
- System composition: neutral thread and “WonderLoom arranged.”

## Motion

- Predetermined UI transitions use CSS and a strong ease-out curve.
- Press feedback scales to 0.97 over roughly 160ms.
- Story cards enter from their spatial source; they never appear from nowhere.
- Canvas reveals use a slow paper-unfurl; transforms use a localized wash rather than a full-screen reset.
- Reduced-motion removes travel, parallax, and scale while preserving opacity and state clarity.

## Never

- Generic gradient-heavy AI aesthetics.
- Chat bubbles as the main experience.
- Hidden authorship, automatic story replacement, or unreviewable AI prose.
- Psychological inference, diagnostic language, resilience scoring, or moralized choices.
- Dark patterns, public profiles, social sharing, surveillance dashboards, or behavioral ranking.
